Overview

Our research focus is on innovative systems and procedures for diagnostics and medical monitoring. The aim is to develop user-friendly, powerful and sustainable solutions. To do this, we research new sensor principles and sensor systems, but also further develop and supplement established processes.

"From signals to insight"

Work Areas

Two closely linked research domains form the core of our activities.

Biomechanical & Electrophysiological Movement Analysis

We design, develop, and test new ways to collect diagnostically relevant information using innovative sensor systems and measurement techniques. Both established approaches in novel settings and new sensor principles are employed, with a focus on biomechanical and electrophysiological signals.

Electrophysiological Brain Activity Analysis

Movement and brain function are deeply interconnected. We integrate movement sequences and brain activity to explore their mutual interactions—both in physiological and pathological contexts—with the long-term goal of developing novel diagnostic and therapeutic methods.

Methods & Equipment

"Generic concepts, versatile applications"

Our research leverages expertise in signal analysis of human gait, fine motor skills, and underlying brain correlates. We utilize a wide range of measurement techniques including:

  • Electroencephalography (EEG)
  • Electromyography (EMG)
  • Wearable sensors
  • Graphics tablets

These tools enable experimental work in both controlled laboratory environments and real-world natural settings, with applications in neurology, cardiology, sports, and exercise science.
