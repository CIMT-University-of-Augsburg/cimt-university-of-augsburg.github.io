Research Focus

The Chair of Informatics for Medical Technologies (CIMT) develops advanced computational methods for the analysis of multimodal neurophysiological and neuroimaging data. Our work bridges signal processing, statistics, and artificial intelligence to uncover biomarkers of neurological and motor disorders.

Core Methodologies

Time Series & Oscillatory Analysis

Mathematical modeling of neural oscillations in sleep and motor systems using time-frequency, phase, and coherence analyses across EEG, MEG, and EMG.

Source & Network Analysis

Source reconstruction (e.g., LCMV beamforming), functional and effective connectivity (WPLI, Granger), and graph-theoretical analyses on fMRI, PET, EEG, and MEG.

Multimodal Integration

Joint analysis of structural (sMRI, DTI) and functional (fMRI, PET, EEG) data to map brain-behavior relationships in health and disease.

Machine Learning & Biomarkers

Deep learning (e.g., BBTransformer) and statistical learning for predictive modeling of clinical traits and discovery of robust neurophysiological biomarkers.
